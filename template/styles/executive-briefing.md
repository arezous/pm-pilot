# Communication Style: Executive Briefing

## Format Rules

- **Length:** Maximum 3 paragraphs
- **Structure:**
  - Paragraph 1: What happened (outcomes, metrics)
  - Paragraph 2: Why it matters (business impact)
  - Paragraph 3: What's next (forward-looking)
- **Metrics:** Use bullet points
- **Tone:** No jargon, active voice
- **Voice:** Confident, direct, outcome-focused

## Template

**[TITLE: Brief descriptive headline]**

**What happened:**
[1-2 sentences summarizing the outcome or decision. Include key metrics as bullets:]
- [Metric 1]
- [Metric 2]
- [Metric 3]

**Why it matters:**
[2-3 sentences on business impact. Connect to company goals, revenue, or strategic priorities. Quantify impact where possible.]

**What's next:**
[2-3 sentences on forward-looking actions. Include owners and timeframes. End with clear next milestone.]

## Example

**User Research Complete: 5 Critical Pain Points Identified**

**What happened:**
We completed 8 user interviews across enterprise admins, engineers, designers, and PMs. The research revealed clear patterns in user frustration:
- 100% of users requested dark mode
- 88% reported notification overload (40-70 notifications/day)
- 100% cited inadequate mobile experience

**Why it matters:**
These pain points directly impact our activation rate (currently 45%, target 60%). Users abandon onboarding due to the "blank screen" problem, and notification fatigue causes users to disengage entirely. Fixing these issues could recover 15+ percentage points in activation and improve retention among engineering teams.

**What's next:**
Product will draft PRDs for dark mode and template library by Friday. Engineering estimates dark mode at 2 sprints. We'll present the full Q1 roadmap recommendation at next week's leadership sync.

## When to Use

- Weekly executive updates
- Board meeting summaries
- Project milestone communications
- Decision announcements to leadership
- Quarterly business reviews
